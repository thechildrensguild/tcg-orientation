👗 Appearance

You will be representing The Children's Guild from your first day. Please dress in a way that is both professional and comfortable so you can do the work and present yourself well.

If you work directly with children and families, the following are required:

👕 Clothing

👟  Shoes: Closed-toe only — sneakers, flats, or loafers. No sandals, flip-flops, or open-back heels.
👕  Fit: Modest — no exposed midriffs, low-cut tops, sheer fabrics, or short shorts/skirts.
🚫  Graphics: Family-friendly — no profanity, drug or alcohol references, political slogans, or graphics unsuitable for children.
🧢  Head: No hats or head coverings indoors (HR can approve religious or medical exceptions in advance).

💎 Accessories & Personal

💍  Jewelry: Minimal — no long necklaces, dangling earrings, or large rings. They can be grabbed during de-escalation.
💅  Nails: Short enough to make a fist comfortably.
🎨  Tattoos: Cover anything unsuitable for children (profanity, weapons, drug or alcohol references). Other tattoos may remain visible.
🌸  Fragrance: None — no perfume, cologne, or strongly scented products. Many children have sensory sensitivities or allergies.

If you work in an admin role and do not work directly with children and families, please keep things business casual and use professional judgment. The guidelines above are still good to keep in mind — you may be asked to visit a school, take a tour, or join a team meeting on short notice.

🧥  Rooms can run cool — bring layers or a jacket.
 

🪪 Required ID Documents for Your 1st Day

Tonya Morse or Kim Allen from HR will meet with you in person or virtually to review your documents and complete the I-9 process. Please bring your original documents — no photocopies.

📘  Option A — one document: U.S. Passport, Green Card, or Work Permit (EAD).
📂  Option B — two documents: Photo ID (Driver's License or State ID) and proof of work eligibility (Social Security card or birth certificate with seal).
