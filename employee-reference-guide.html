At The Children's Guild, every employee and every position matters because together, we can achieve our goal of helping children and families to thrive.

About This Employee Reference Guide▼

This Employee Reference Guide replaces all prior handbooks, and any other description of practices and benefits of The Children's Guild. Obviously, The Children's Guild cannot explain every Company policy, rule, or benefit in this reference guide. Its provisions are no more than general summaries of the benefits, work rules and policies they address. The Children's Guild may unilaterally amend, supplement, modify, or eliminate one or more of the benefits, work rules or policies described in this handbook, or any other employment benefits, work rules or policies, without prior notice. All employees are responsible for becoming familiar with Company policies.

Employment with The Children's Guild is deemed "at will," which means that it is voluntarily entered into and the employee is free to resign at will at any time, with or without cause. Similarly, The Children's Guild may end the employment relationship at will at any time, with or without notice or cause. The Children's Guild adheres to all applicable employment laws.

Policies set forth in this reference guide are neither intended to create a contract, nor are they to be construed to constitute contractual obligations of any kind, or a contract of employment between The Children's Guild and any of its employees.

Employment

Equal Employment Opportunity▼

The Children's Guild is committed to providing equal employment opportunities to all employees and applicants without regard to race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, marital status, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, veteran status, or any other characteristic protected by applicable law. This policy applies to all terms and conditions of employment, including hiring, placement, promotion, termination, layoff, recall, transfer, leaves of absence, compensation, and training.

The Children's Guild expressly prohibits any form of workplace harassment based on any protected characteristic. Improper interference with the ability of employees to perform their job duties may result in discipline up to and including discharge.

Equal Pay▼

The Children's Guild is committed to the principle of equal pay for equal work. Compensation is determined by job responsibilities, qualifications, experience, performance, and market conditions — not by sex, gender identity, race, or any other protected characteristic. If you believe there is a pay discrepancy, you are encouraged to bring it to the attention of Human Resources for review.

Employees have the right to discuss their wages with coworkers. The Children's Guild will not retaliate against any employee for inquiring about, discussing, or disclosing their wages or the wages of another employee.

Immigration Law Compliance▼

The Children's Guild is committed to employing only United States citizens and aliens who are authorized to work in the United States. In compliance with the Immigration Reform and Control Act of 1986, each new employee, as a condition of employment, must complete the Employment Eligibility Verification Form I-9 and present documentation establishing identity and employment eligibility.

Former employees who are rehired must also complete the form if they have not completed an I-9 with The Children's Guild within the past three years, or if their previous I-9 is no longer retained or valid. Employees with questions or seeking more information on immigration law issues are encouraged to contact Human Resources.

Disability Accommodations▼

The Children's Guild complies with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and applicable state and local laws providing for nondiscrimination in employment against qualified individuals with disabilities. The Children's Guild also provides reasonable accommodation for such individuals in accordance with these laws.

Reasonable accommodations may include modifications to the work environment, job restructuring, modified work schedules, reassignment to a vacant position, or other adjustments. Employees who need accommodation should contact Human Resources to begin the interactive accommodation process. All requests are evaluated on a case-by-case basis.

Onboarding, Orientation and Training▼

The Children's Guild provides a comprehensive onboarding experience for all new employees. The onboarding process includes:

  • New Hire Orientation covering organizational history, mission, vision, and core values
  • Required compliance training including mandated reporter training, HIPAA, and workplace safety
  • Program-specific training relevant to your role and work location
  • Introduction to key policies, benefits, and resources
  • Meeting your team and understanding your role within the organization

    • All required training must be completed within the timeframes specified during orientation. Ongoing professional development opportunities are available through The Children's Guild's learning management system and in-person training sessions.

Employment Verifications▼

All inquiries regarding current or former employees must be directed to Human Resources. No employee, supervisor, or manager should provide employment verification or reference information on behalf of The Children's Guild without authorization from Human Resources.

Human Resources will confirm dates of employment, job title, and salary information only with written authorization from the employee. Additional information may be released with the employee's written consent.

Performance Review▼

The Children's Guild conducts performance reviews to evaluate employee performance, set goals, and identify professional development opportunities. Performance reviews provide an opportunity for open dialogue between employees and supervisors about job performance, expectations, and career growth.

The review process includes:

  • Annual performance evaluations
  • Goal setting and progress check-ins
  • Discussion of strengths and areas for development
  • Professional development planning

    • Employees are encouraged to participate actively in the review process and to discuss their career aspirations with their supervisors.

Disciplinary Actions▼

The Children's Guild uses a progressive discipline approach to address performance issues and policy violations. The goal of progressive discipline is to correct behavior and help employees succeed, not to punish.

Progressive discipline steps may include:

  • Verbal warning/counseling
  • Written warning
  • Final written warning or suspension
  • Termination

    • The Children's Guild reserves the right to skip steps in the progressive discipline process depending on the severity of the infraction. Some violations may result in immediate termination. All disciplinary actions are documented and placed in the employee's personnel file.

Employee Recognition Awards▼

The Children's Guild values the contributions of its employees and recognizes outstanding performance through various recognition programs. These programs celebrate employees who demonstrate exceptional dedication, innovation, and commitment to the organization's mission.

Recognition opportunities include:

  • Employee of the Month/Quarter
  • Annual awards ceremony
  • Peer-to-peer recognition
  • Years of service milestones
  • Special achievement recognition

    • All employees are eligible for recognition. Nominations can be submitted by supervisors, peers, or other staff members.

Personal Information▼

Employees are responsible for keeping their personal information up to date in the Human Resources Information System (HRIS). This includes:

  • Legal name and address changes
  • Phone number and email updates
  • Emergency contact information
  • Tax withholding changes (W-4)
  • Banking information for direct deposit
  • Dependent and beneficiary changes

    • Changes can be made through the employee self-service portal in Paycom or by contacting Human Resources directly. Keeping information current ensures you receive important communications and that your benefits and payroll are processed correctly.

Employee Files▼

The Children's Guild maintains confidential personnel files for all employees. These files contain employment-related documents including applications, performance reviews, disciplinary records, and benefits information.

Employee files are the property of The Children's Guild and are kept confidential. Access is limited to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis. Employees may review their own personnel file by scheduling an appointment with Human Resources. Files must be reviewed in the presence of an HR representative and may not be removed from the HR office.

Personal Property▼

The Children's Guild is not responsible for loss, damage, or theft of personal property brought to the workplace. Employees are encouraged to take precautions to safeguard their personal belongings, including:

  • Keeping valuables secured and out of sight
  • Locking vehicles in the parking lot
  • Not bringing unnecessary valuables to work
  • Reporting any theft or suspicious activity immediately

    • The Children's Guild provides secure storage areas where available, but assumes no responsibility for personal items.

Cell Phone and Credit Card Usage▼

Employees who are issued company cell phones or credit cards are expected to use them responsibly and for business purposes only. Personal use of company-issued devices and credit cards is prohibited unless otherwise authorized by management.

Cell Phone Guidelines:

  • Use company phones primarily for work-related communication
  • Follow safe driving practices — do not use phones while driving
  • Report lost or stolen devices immediately
  • Return all devices upon separation from employment

    • Credit Card Guidelines:

    • Use for approved business expenses only
    • Submit receipts and expense reports within required timeframes
    • Follow purchasing approval procedures
    • Report lost or stolen cards immediately
Lost or Missing Badges▼

Employee identification badges are required for building access and security purposes. If your badge is lost, stolen, or damaged, you must report it to your supervisor and Human Resources immediately.

A replacement badge will be issued as quickly as possible. Replacement badges may be subject to a replacement fee. For security purposes, lost badges are deactivated upon report. Never share your badge with others or use another employee's badge for building access.

Workplace Monitoring▼

The Children's Guild reserves the right to monitor workplace activities to ensure safety, security, and compliance with company policies. Monitoring may include:

  • Security cameras in common areas and entrances
  • Email and internet usage on company systems
  • Computer and network activity
  • Phone usage on company lines

    • Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using company equipment, systems, or facilities. All electronic communications created, sent, or received using company systems are the property of The Children's Guild and may be accessed at any time.

Licensure and Certification▼

Employees in positions that require professional licensure, certification, or registration are responsible for maintaining current and valid credentials at all times. This includes but is not limited to:

  • Teaching certificates
  • Clinical licenses (LCSW, LCPC, etc.)
  • Nursing licenses
  • CPR/First Aid certifications
  • Driver's licenses (for positions requiring driving)

    • Employees must provide copies of current credentials to Human Resources and notify HR immediately of any changes in credential status, including expiration, renewal, suspension, or revocation. Failure to maintain required credentials may result in reassignment or termination.

Termination of Employment▼

Employment with The Children's Guild may end through voluntary resignation, involuntary termination, retirement, or other means. Employees who choose to resign are encouraged to provide at least two weeks' written notice to allow for a smooth transition.

Upon separation from employment:

  • Return all company property including keys, badges, equipment, and documents
  • Complete an exit interview with Human Resources
  • Final paychecks will be issued in accordance with applicable state law
  • Benefits continuation information (COBRA) will be provided
  • Accrued but unused PTO will be paid out according to policy

    • The Children's Guild conducts exit interviews to gather feedback and ensure a positive transition for departing employees.

Employee Relations▼

The Children's Guild maintains an open-door policy and encourages employees to share their concerns, suggestions, and questions with their supervisors or Human Resources. If you have a workplace concern:

  • Discuss the issue with your direct supervisor first
  • If the issue is not resolved or you are uncomfortable going to your supervisor, contact Human Resources
  • You may also reach out to the Employee Assistance Program (EAP) for confidential support

    • The Children's Guild is committed to addressing employee concerns promptly and fairly. Retaliation against any employee for raising a concern in good faith is strictly prohibited.

Benefit Eligibility▼

Full-time employees who work 30 or more hours per week are eligible for The Children's Guild's benefits package. Benefits enrollment information is provided during the onboarding process.

Key eligibility information:

  • Benefits effective date is outlined in your enrollment materials
  • Open enrollment occurs annually, typically in the fall
  • Qualifying life events (marriage, birth, etc.) may allow mid-year changes
  • Part-time employees may be eligible for limited benefits

    • Detailed information about each benefit plan is available through Human Resources and the employee self-service portal.

Health, Vision, and Dental Insurance▼

The Children's Guild offers comprehensive health, vision, and dental insurance plans for eligible employees and their dependents. Multiple plan options are available to meet different needs and budgets.

Health Insurance:

  • Multiple plan options with varying levels of coverage
  • In-network and out-of-network coverage
  • Prescription drug benefits included

    • Vision Insurance:

    • Annual eye exams
    • Allowances for frames and lenses or contact lenses

      • Dental Insurance:

      • Preventive care covered at 100%
      • Basic and major services at varying coverage levels
      • Orthodontic benefits available on select plans

        • Detailed plan information, costs, and enrollment instructions are provided during open enrollment and new hire onboarding.

Flexible Spending Account (FSA)▼

The Children's Guild offers Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs) that allow employees to set aside pre-tax dollars to pay for eligible healthcare and dependent care expenses.

Healthcare FSA:

  • Pay for eligible medical, dental, and vision expenses not covered by insurance
  • Reduces taxable income
  • Use-it-or-lose-it rules apply (with applicable grace period or rollover)

    • Dependent Care FSA:

    • Pay for eligible childcare or dependent care expenses
    • Available for children under 13 or disabled dependents
    • Maximum annual contribution limits apply

      • FSA elections are made during open enrollment and cannot be changed mid-year unless you experience a qualifying life event.

COBRA▼

Under the Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act (COBRA), employees and their covered dependents who lose health coverage due to a qualifying event may continue their group health benefits for a limited period of time.

Qualifying events include:

  • Termination of employment (voluntary or involuntary, except for gross misconduct)
  • Reduction in hours
  • Divorce or legal separation
  • Death of covered employee
  • Dependent child losing eligibility

    • COBRA coverage is typically available for 18-36 months depending on the qualifying event. The employee is responsible for the full cost of coverage plus a 2% administrative fee. COBRA election information is provided at the time of the qualifying event.

Supplemental Insurance▼

The Children's Guild offers optional supplemental insurance plans to provide additional financial protection. These voluntary benefits are available at group rates and are paid for by the employee through payroll deduction.

Available supplemental plans may include:

  • Accident Insurance — provides cash benefits for covered accidents
  • Critical Illness Insurance — lump sum payment for qualifying diagnoses
  • Hospital Indemnity Insurance — daily cash benefit for hospital stays

    • Supplemental insurance plans pay benefits directly to you, regardless of other insurance coverage. Enrollment is available during open enrollment or as a new hire.

Life Insurance▼

The Children's Guild provides basic life insurance coverage at no cost to eligible employees. The basic life insurance benefit is equal to one times your annual salary (up to applicable limits).

Additional coverage options:

  • Voluntary supplemental life insurance for yourself (at group rates)
  • Spouse/domestic partner life insurance
  • Dependent child life insurance

    • Voluntary life insurance is paid for by the employee through payroll deduction. Evidence of insurability may be required for coverage above guaranteed issue amounts. Beneficiary designations should be kept up to date through the employee self-service portal.

Retirement Plan (403b)▼

The Children's Guild offers a 403(b) retirement savings plan to help employees save for the future. Key features include:

  • Pre-tax and Roth (after-tax) contribution options
  • Employer matching contributions (details provided during enrollment)
  • Wide selection of investment options
  • Loans and hardship withdrawals may be available
  • Automatic payroll deductions for easy saving

    • New employees are eligible to participate according to the plan's eligibility requirements. Contribution limits are set annually by the IRS. Employees are encouraged to contribute enough to take full advantage of any employer match. Retirement plan details and enrollment information are available through Human Resources.

Short-Term and Long-Term Disability▼

The Children's Guild provides disability insurance coverage to protect employees who are unable to work due to a qualifying illness or injury.

Short-Term Disability (STD):

  • Provides partial income replacement for temporary disabilities
  • Waiting period applies before benefits begin
  • Typically covers up to 26 weeks

    • Long-Term Disability (LTD):

    • Begins after short-term disability benefits are exhausted
    • Provides partial income replacement for extended disabilities
    • Benefits may continue until recovery, retirement age, or maximum benefit period

      • Disability claims require medical documentation. Contact Human Resources for information about filing a disability claim and coordinating with other leave programs like FMLA.

Credit Union▼

The Children's Guild employees are eligible for membership in a credit union, which offers a variety of financial services at competitive rates. Benefits of credit union membership may include:

  • Savings and checking accounts
  • Competitive loan rates (auto, personal, mortgage)
  • Financial counseling and education resources
  • Online and mobile banking
  • Direct deposit capabilities

    • Information about credit union membership and enrollment is available through Human Resources or during new hire orientation.

Workers' Compensation▼

The Children's Guild provides workers' compensation insurance for employees who sustain work-related injuries or illnesses. Workers' compensation covers:

  • Medical expenses related to the work injury
  • Partial wage replacement during recovery
  • Rehabilitation services
  • Disability benefits if applicable

    • If you are injured on the job:

    1. Report the injury to your supervisor immediately
    2. Seek medical attention as needed
    3. Complete an incident report
    4. Contact Human Resources to file a workers' compensation claim

    Prompt reporting is essential for processing your claim. Failure to report a work-related injury in a timely manner may affect your eligibility for benefits.

Unemployment Compensation▼

Employees who are separated from employment through no fault of their own may be eligible for unemployment compensation benefits. Eligibility is determined by the applicable state unemployment agency based on factors including:

  • Reason for separation
  • Length of employment
  • Wages earned

    • The Children's Guild pays unemployment insurance taxes on behalf of all employees. If you believe you are eligible for unemployment benefits, contact your state's unemployment office to file a claim. Human Resources can provide the information needed for your claim.

Employee Assistance Program (EAP)▼

The Children's Guild provides an Employee Assistance Program (EAP) at no cost to employees and their immediate family members. The EAP offers confidential support services including:

  • Short-term counseling (in-person, phone, or virtual)
  • Referrals for longer-term treatment
  • Legal consultation and referrals
  • Financial counseling and planning resources
  • Work-life balance resources
  • Substance abuse support
  • Crisis intervention

    • EAP services are completely confidential. The Children's Guild does not receive information about who uses the program or the nature of their concerns. Contact information for the EAP is provided during orientation and is available from Human Resources.

Jury Duty▼

The Children's Guild supports employees who are called to serve on jury duty. If you receive a jury summons:

  • Notify your supervisor as soon as possible
  • Provide a copy of the summons to Human Resources
  • You will receive your regular pay for a limited period of jury service (minus any jury duty compensation received from the court)
  • You are expected to return to work on any day you are dismissed early from jury duty

    • Employees will not be penalized or retaliated against for serving on jury duty.

Election Day▼

The Children's Guild encourages all employees to exercise their right to vote. If your work schedule does not allow sufficient time to vote before or after working hours, The Children's Guild will provide time off in accordance with applicable state election laws.

Employees who need time off to vote should notify their supervisor in advance. You may be asked to provide proof that you voted or attempted to vote.

Working Hours▼

Standard working hours and schedules are determined by position, program, and operational needs. Your supervisor will communicate your expected work schedule, including start and end times, break periods, and any schedule variations.

General guidelines:

  • The standard workweek runs from Sunday through Saturday
  • Full-time employees typically work 37.5-40 hours per week
  • Break and meal periods are provided in accordance with applicable law
  • Schedule changes must be approved by your supervisor

    • Flexible scheduling may be available for some positions with supervisor approval. Employees are expected to report to work on time and ready to perform their duties.

Overtime Work▼

Non-exempt employees who work more than 40 hours in a workweek are entitled to overtime pay at a rate of one and one-half times their regular hourly rate. Key overtime guidelines:

  • All overtime must be pre-approved by your supervisor
  • Unauthorized overtime may result in disciplinary action
  • Overtime is calculated based on actual hours worked in the workweek
  • Paid time off (PTO, holidays) does not count as "hours worked" for overtime calculation

    • Exempt (salaried) employees are not eligible for overtime pay. If you have questions about your classification or overtime eligibility, contact Human Resources.

Payroll
5▼
Pay Schedule▼

The Children's Guild pays employees on a bi-weekly basis (every two weeks). Key payroll information:

  • Pay periods run from Sunday through Saturday
  • Paychecks/direct deposits are issued on the Friday following the end of each pay period
  • If a pay date falls on a holiday, payments are typically issued the preceding business day
  • Pay stubs are available through the Paycom employee self-service portal

    • A payroll calendar with all pay dates is published annually and available through Human Resources and the employee portal.

10/11/12 Month Pay▼

Depending on your position and program, you may be on a 10-month, 11-month, or 12-month pay schedule:

  • 10-Month Employees: Work during the school year. May elect to have salary spread over 12 months for consistent pay year-round or receive pay only during working months.
  • 11-Month Employees: Work during the school year plus additional time (e.g., summer programming).
  • 12-Month Employees: Work year-round with standard scheduling.

    • Your offer letter specifies your pay schedule. If you have questions about your pay schedule or options for spreading pay, contact Human Resources or Payroll.

Timesheets▼

All non-exempt (hourly) employees are required to accurately record their work hours each pay period using the Paycom time and attendance system.

Timesheet requirements:

  • Clock in and out at the start and end of each shift
  • Record meal breaks accurately
  • Submit timesheets by the deadline each pay period
  • Report any timesheet errors to your supervisor immediately

    • Supervisors are responsible for reviewing and approving employee timesheets by the payroll deadline. Falsification of time records is a serious offense that may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

    Exempt (salaried) employees may also be required to track time for project management or grant reporting purposes.

Approve My Check▼

Each pay period, employees should review their paycheck details through the Paycom self-service portal to ensure accuracy. The "Approve My Check" feature allows you to:

  • Review hours worked and pay rate
  • Verify deductions (taxes, benefits, retirement)
  • Confirm direct deposit amounts
  • Flag any discrepancies before payment is processed

    • If you notice any errors in your pay, contact your supervisor and the Payroll department immediately. Corrections will be made as soon as possible, typically on the next pay cycle. Timely review helps ensure you receive accurate compensation.

Direct Deposit▼

The Children's Guild offers direct deposit for all employees and strongly encourages its use. Direct deposit provides:

  • Faster access to your pay
  • Elimination of paper checks
  • Ability to split deposits across multiple accounts
  • Secure and reliable payment

    • To set up or modify direct deposit:

    1. Log into Paycom self-service portal
    2. Navigate to the direct deposit section
    3. Enter your bank routing and account numbers
    4. Specify deposit amounts or percentages

    Changes to direct deposit may take one pay cycle to take effect. During the transition, you may receive a paper check.

Paid Holiday Schedule▼

The Children's Guild observes designated paid holidays each year. The annual holiday schedule is published by Human Resources and may include:

  • New Year's Day
  • Martin Luther King Jr. Day
  • Presidents' Day
  • Memorial Day
  • Juneteenth
  • Independence Day
  • Labor Day
  • Columbus Day / Indigenous Peoples' Day
  • Veterans Day
  • Thanksgiving Day and the day after
  • Christmas Eve and Christmas Day
  • Additional days as designated by the organization

    • Holiday schedules may vary by program and work location. Some programs may observe additional or alternative holidays based on operational needs. The official holiday calendar is distributed annually.

Blackout Days▼

The Children's Guild may designate certain periods as "blackout days" during which paid time off (PTO) requests generally cannot be approved. Blackout periods are established based on operational needs and may include:

  • First and last weeks of the school year
  • Key training or professional development days
  • Critical program dates
  • Other periods as determined by program leadership

    • Blackout dates are communicated in advance by your supervisor or program director. Exceptions to blackout periods may be granted on a case-by-case basis for emergency or extenuating circumstances, with supervisor and HR approval.

Bereavement Leave▼

The Children's Guild provides paid bereavement leave to employees who experience the death of a family member. Bereavement leave allows time to grieve, make arrangements, and attend services.

  • Immediate family (spouse, child, parent, sibling): Up to 5 days
  • Extended family (grandparent, grandchild, in-law, aunt, uncle): Up to 3 days
  • Close friend or colleague: Up to 1 day

    • Additional time off may be taken using PTO or unpaid leave with supervisor approval. The Children's Guild may request documentation such as an obituary or funeral program. Notify your supervisor as soon as possible when you need bereavement leave.

Paid Time Off (PTO)▼

The Children's Guild provides Paid Time Off (PTO) to eligible employees for vacation, personal time, or other time away from work. PTO accrual rates are based on your length of service and employment status.

Key PTO guidelines:

  • PTO must be requested and approved in advance through Paycom
  • Provide as much notice as possible for planned time off
  • PTO requests are subject to supervisor approval and operational needs
  • Unused PTO may be carried over to the next year (up to applicable limits)
  • PTO accrual rates increase with years of service

    • Refer to the PTO Accrual Rates section for specific accrual information based on your length of service.

PTO Accrual Rates▼

PTO accrues each pay period based on your years of service with The Children's Guild. Accrual begins on your date of hire.

Typical accrual schedule for full-time employees:

  • 0-2 years: Accrues at a base rate
  • 3-5 years: Increased accrual rate
  • 6-10 years: Further increased accrual rate
  • 10+ years: Maximum accrual rate

    • Important notes:

    • PTO accrues on a per-pay-period basis
    • Accrual rates are prorated for part-time employees
    • There is a maximum PTO balance (cap) — accrual stops until PTO is used
    • Specific accrual amounts are outlined in your offer letter and benefits materials

      • Contact Human Resources for detailed information about your specific accrual rate.

Leave Payouts▼

Upon separation from employment, accrued but unused PTO is paid out in accordance with The Children's Guild policy and applicable state law.

Payout guidelines:

  • Accrued, unused PTO will be included in your final paycheck
  • Payout is calculated at your current regular rate of pay
  • Employees who provide appropriate notice are eligible for full PTO payout
  • Sick leave balances are generally not paid out upon separation

    • Final paychecks including PTO payouts are issued in accordance with applicable state wage payment laws. Contact Human Resources for questions about your specific payout eligibility.

Sick and Safe Leave▼

The Children's Guild provides sick and safe leave in compliance with applicable Maryland and Washington, D.C. laws. Sick and safe leave may be used for:

  • Your own illness, injury, or medical condition
  • Medical appointments for yourself or a family member
  • Care for a sick family member
  • Needs related to domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking (safe leave)
  • Public health emergencies or school/business closures

    • Sick and safe leave accrues according to applicable law. Employees must follow call-out procedures and may be required to provide medical documentation for absences exceeding three consecutive days. Unused sick leave does not typically pay out upon separation.

Family Member Definition▼

For purposes of sick leave and other leave policies, "family member" is broadly defined to include:

  • Spouse or domestic partner
  • Child (biological, adopted, foster, stepchild, legal ward)
  • Parent (biological, adoptive, foster, stepparent, in-law)
  • Grandparent or grandchild
  • Sibling
  • Any individual related by blood or affinity whose close association is the equivalent of a family relationship

    • This definition applies to sick and safe leave, bereavement leave, and FMLA leave where applicable. Specific leave types may have different definitions of eligible family members as required by law.

Military Leave▼

The Children's Guild complies with the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA) and applicable state military leave laws. Employees who serve in the uniformed services are entitled to:

  • Leave of absence for military service, training, or related obligations
  • Reemployment rights upon return from service
  • Continuation of health benefits during military leave
  • Protection from discrimination based on military service

    • Employees should provide advance notice of military service when possible. Upon return from military leave, employees are entitled to be reemployed in the position they would have attained had they not been absent for military service, with the same seniority, status, and pay.

Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)▼

The Children's Guild complies with the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA), which provides eligible employees with up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per 12-month period for qualifying reasons:

  • Birth of a child and bonding with newborn
  • Placement of a child for adoption or foster care
  • Care for a spouse, child, or parent with a serious health condition
  • Your own serious health condition that makes you unable to perform your job
  • Qualifying exigency related to a family member's military service

    • Eligibility requirements:

    • 12 months of employment with TCG
    • 1,250 hours worked in the previous 12 months

      • Employees may be required to use accrued PTO concurrently with FMLA leave. Health benefits are maintained during FMLA leave. Contact Human Resources for detailed information and to begin the leave request process.

Organ Donation Leave▼

The Children's Guild provides leave for employees who serve as organ or bone marrow donors in accordance with applicable state law.

  • Organ donation: Up to 60 days of paid leave (per Maryland law)
  • Bone marrow donation: Up to 7 days of paid leave

    • Employees must provide medical documentation supporting the need for donation leave. This leave is in addition to any other leave benefits and does not count against PTO or sick leave balances. The Children's Guild will not retaliate against any employee for exercising their right to organ donation leave.

Maryland Unpaid Parental Leave▼

In addition to FMLA, eligible employees in Maryland may be entitled to unpaid parental leave under the Maryland Flexible Leave Act and other applicable state laws.

This leave may be used for:

  • Birth of a child
  • Adoption or foster care placement
  • Bonding time with a new child

    • Eligibility and duration are determined by applicable state law. This leave may run concurrently with FMLA leave if the employee is eligible for both. Employees should contact Human Resources for information about their specific eligibility and to coordinate leave requests.

Unpaid Leave of Absence▼

Employees who have exhausted all applicable paid leave and FMLA leave may request an unpaid leave of absence for personal reasons. Key guidelines:

  • Requests must be submitted in writing to your supervisor and Human Resources
  • Approval is at the discretion of management based on operational needs
  • Duration is typically limited (consult HR for specific limits)
  • Benefits continuation during unpaid leave varies — contact HR for details
  • Employees must return to work on the agreed-upon date or contact HR to request an extension
  • Failure to return from an approved leave may be treated as voluntary resignation

    • The Children's Guild will make reasonable efforts to hold the employee's position during an approved unpaid leave, but cannot guarantee the same position will be available upon return.

Workplace Standards
6▼
Attendance and Supervision▼

Regular and punctual attendance is essential to the smooth operation of The Children's Guild's programs and services. Employees are expected to:

  • Report to work on time as scheduled
  • Follow established call-out procedures when unable to work
  • Notify your supervisor as early as possible if you will be absent or late
  • Provide documentation for absences when requested

    • Excessive absenteeism or tardiness may result in disciplinary action. Supervisors are responsible for tracking attendance and addressing attendance concerns with employees in a timely manner. If you have a medical condition affecting attendance, contact Human Resources to discuss possible accommodations.

Personal Appearance▼

The Children's Guild expects all employees to present a professional appearance appropriate for their role and work environment. General guidelines:

  • Dress should be clean, neat, and appropriate for your position
  • Program-specific dress codes may apply (e.g., clinical settings, school environments)
  • Safety gear and protective equipment must be worn when required
  • Clothing with offensive messages or graphics is not permitted
  • Good personal hygiene is expected

    • Supervisors may send employees home to change if their appearance does not meet workplace standards. If you have questions about the dress code for your specific program or position, consult your supervisor. Religious and disability-related accommodations will be provided in accordance with applicable law.

Standards of Conduct▼

The Children's Guild expects all employees to maintain high standards of professional and ethical conduct. This includes:

  • Treating all children, families, colleagues, and community members with respect and dignity
  • Maintaining professional boundaries at all times
  • Following all organizational policies and procedures
  • Complying with applicable laws and regulations
  • Reporting concerns about unethical or inappropriate behavior
  • Protecting confidential information
  • Being honest and transparent in all professional interactions

    • Violations of the standards of conduct may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination. Employees are encouraged to report concerns through appropriate channels without fear of retaliation.

Drug and Smoke-Free Workplace▼

The Children's Guild is committed to maintaining a drug-free and smoke-free workplace to ensure the health, safety, and well-being of all employees and those we serve.

Drug-Free Policy:

  • The use, possession, distribution, or being under the influence of illegal drugs or alcohol on company premises or during work hours is strictly prohibited
  • Prescription medications that may impair job performance must be reported to your supervisor or Human Resources
  • Drug and alcohol testing may be conducted as permitted by law

    • Smoke-Free Policy:

    • Smoking (including e-cigarettes and vaping) is prohibited in all TCG buildings, vehicles, and on TCG property
    • Designated smoking areas may be provided at some locations

      • Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination. Employees who need help with substance abuse issues are encouraged to contact the Employee Assistance Program (EAP).

Vehicle Use▼

Employees who use personal or company vehicles for work-related purposes must comply with the following requirements:

  • Maintain a valid driver's license appropriate for the vehicle being operated
  • Maintain personal auto insurance that meets minimum state requirements
  • Follow all traffic laws and safe driving practices
  • Report any accidents, moving violations, or license suspensions to Human Resources
  • Never use a cell phone while driving (hands-free devices may be permitted where legal)
  • Never operate a vehicle while under the influence of drugs or alcohol

    • Company Vehicle Guidelines:

    • Company vehicles are for authorized business use only
    • Complete vehicle inspection logs as required
    • Report any maintenance needs or damage immediately
    • Return vehicles clean and with adequate fuel
Workplace Violence▼

The Children's Guild has zero tolerance for workplace violence. This includes any act of violence, threat of violence, intimidation, bullying, or harassment that occurs in the workplace or in connection with work activities.

Prohibited conduct includes:

  • Physical assault or battery
  • Verbal or written threats of violence
  • Intimidating or threatening behavior
  • Possession of weapons on company premises
  • Stalking or harassment
  • Destruction of property

    • If you witness or experience workplace violence:

    1. Remove yourself from immediate danger
    2. Call 911 if there is an immediate threat
    3. Report the incident to your supervisor and Human Resources
    4. Complete an incident report

    All reports of workplace violence will be taken seriously and investigated promptly. Retaliation against anyone who reports workplace violence in good faith is strictly prohibited.

Computer, Email, and Internet Usage▼

The Children's Guild provides computer equipment, email, internet access, and other technology resources for business purposes. Guidelines for appropriate use:

  • Company technology is primarily for work-related activities
  • Limited personal use may be permitted if it does not interfere with work duties
  • All electronic communications on company systems may be monitored
  • Employees have no expectation of privacy on company systems
  • Do not download unauthorized software or access inappropriate content

Social Networking▼

The Children's Guild recognizes that employees may use social media in their personal lives. When doing so, employees must exercise good judgment and follow these guidelines:

  • Never share confidential information about TCG, its programs, clients, or employees
  • Never post photos, videos, or information about the children and families we serve
  • Do not represent yourself as speaking on behalf of The Children's Guild without authorization
  • Be respectful and professional in all online interactions
  • Make clear that personal opinions are your own and do not represent TCG
  • Do not use company time or resources for personal social media activity

All media inquiries and requests for information about The Children's Guild must be directed to authorized organizational spokespersons. Employees should not:

  • Share internal information, documents, or photographs with media
  • Arrange interviews or facility tours without approval

    2. Obtain the reporter's name, outlet, and contact information
    3. Direct the inquiry to the designated communications contact

Safety & Security
5▼
Criminal Background Check▼

As an organization serving vulnerable populations, The Children's Guild requires all employees to pass a criminal background check as a condition of employment. This includes:

  • State and federal criminal history check
  • Sex offender registry check
  • Child abuse and neglect registry check (where applicable)
  • FBI fingerprint check (for applicable positions)

    • Background checks may also be conducted periodically during employment. Employees are required to report any arrests, charges, or convictions to Human Resources immediately. Failure to disclose may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

    Background check results are evaluated on a case-by-case basis considering the nature of the offense, time elapsed, and relevance to the position.

Protective Service Calls▼

All Children's Guild employees are mandated reporters under applicable state laws (Maryland and/or Washington, D.C.). If you suspect that a child is being abused or neglected, you are legally required to report it immediately.

Reporting procedure:

  1. Ensure the immediate safety of the child
  2. Report your concerns to your supervisor immediately
  3. Contact the appropriate protective services agency:
  4. Maryland: Child Protective Services
  5. DC: Child and Family Services Agency (CFSA)
  6. Complete all required internal documentation
  7. Cooperate with any investigation

Key reminders:

  • You do not need to have proof — a reasonable suspicion is sufficient
  • Reports may be made anonymously
  • TCG will not retaliate against employees who make good-faith reports
  • Failure to report suspected abuse or neglect is a criminal offense

    • All employees receive mandated reporter training during orientation and annually thereafter.

Security and Facilities▼

The Children's Guild is committed to maintaining safe and secure facilities for all employees, children, and families. Security guidelines include:

  • Always wear and display your employee ID badge
  • Do not allow unauthorized individuals to enter secured areas (no tailgating)
  • Report any suspicious activity or persons to your supervisor and security
  • Follow visitor management procedures — all visitors must sign in and be escorted
  • Keep doors and gates secured at all times
  • Know the emergency exits and evacuation routes for your building
  • Participate in required safety drills (fire, lockdown, evacuation)

    • Facility guidelines:

    • Report maintenance and safety concerns promptly
    • Keep work areas clean and free of hazards
    • Follow program-specific environmental safety requirements
    • Respect shared spaces and common areas
Inclement Weather▼

During inclement weather or other emergency situations, The Children's Guild follows established procedures for closings, delays, and early dismissals.

How to find out about closings/delays:

  • Check for official communications via email, text, or phone notification system
  • Monitor local news for school closing announcements
  • Check the employee portal or designated communication channels

    • General guidelines:

    • Follow the closing/delay decisions for your specific program and location
    • If TCG is open but you cannot travel safely, contact your supervisor
    • Essential personnel may be required to report even during closings
    • Remote work may be an option for applicable positions during weather events

      • Your safety is the top priority. Use good judgment when deciding whether it is safe to travel to work during severe weather.

Sexual Harassment▼

The Children's Guild is committed to maintaining a workplace free from sexual harassment. Sexual harassment of any kind is strictly prohibited and will not be tolerated.

Sexual harassment includes:

  • Unwelcome sexual advances or requests for sexual favors
  • Verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature that interferes with work performance
  • Creating an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment
  • Quid pro quo harassment (conditioning employment decisions on sexual favors)

    • If you experience or witness sexual harassment:

    1. Tell the harasser to stop, if you feel safe doing so
    2. Report the incident to your supervisor, Human Resources, or any member of management
    3. You may also file a complaint with the EEOC or applicable state agency

    All complaints will be investigated promptly, thoroughly, and confidentially to the extent possible. The Children's Guild will not retaliate against any employee who reports sexual harassment or participates in an investigation in good faith.

    Employees found to have engaged in sexual harassment will be subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination.

Ethics & Compliance
6▼
Confidentiality▼

Protecting confidential information is a fundamental responsibility of every Children's Guild employee. Confidential information includes but is not limited to:

  • Client/student records, treatment plans, and personal information
  • Employee personal and personnel information
  • Financial and business information
  • Proprietary programs, methods, and materials
  • Board and leadership communications

    • Guidelines for protecting confidential information:

    • Only access information you need for your job duties
    • Never share confidential information with unauthorized individuals
    • Secure documents, files, and electronic devices when not in use
    • Follow HIPAA and FERPA regulations as applicable
    • Dispose of confidential materials properly (shredding, secure deletion)
    • Report any breaches or suspected breaches immediately

      • The obligation to protect confidential information continues even after separation from employment. Violations may result in disciplinary action and legal consequences.

Conflict of Interest▼

Employees of The Children's Guild must avoid situations where their personal interests may conflict with the interests of the organization. A conflict of interest exists when personal activities, relationships, or financial interests could impair judgment or interfere with job duties.

Examples of potential conflicts:

  • Outside employment that interferes with TCG duties
  • Financial interest in organizations that do business with TCG
  • Personal relationships that could influence business decisions
  • Using TCG resources or position for personal gain
  • Accepting gifts or favors that could influence professional judgment

    • Employees must disclose potential conflicts of interest to their supervisor and Human Resources. Conflicts will be evaluated on a case-by-case basis, and appropriate measures will be taken to manage or eliminate the conflict.

Ethical Practice▼

The Children's Guild is committed to conducting all operations with the highest standards of ethical conduct. All employees are expected to:

  • Act with integrity and honesty in all professional interactions
  • Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and professional standards
  • Report suspected fraud, waste, abuse, or ethical violations
  • Maintain professional boundaries with clients and families
  • Avoid conflicts of interest
  • Protect organizational resources and use them responsibly
  • Treat all individuals with dignity and respect

    • The Children's Guild has established ethics reporting mechanisms for employees to report concerns. Reports can be made to supervisors, Human Resources, or through the organization's ethics reporting process. Whistleblower protections apply to employees who report concerns in good faith.

Gifts▼

The Children's Guild has guidelines regarding the giving and receiving of gifts to maintain ethical standards and avoid conflicts of interest.

General guidelines:

  • Employees may not accept gifts of significant value from vendors, contractors, or others doing business with TCG
  • Small tokens of appreciation (such as holiday treats or thank-you cards) are generally acceptable
  • Gifts that could create an obligation or appearance of favoritism should be declined
  • Cash gifts should never be accepted
  • Gifts from clients and families should be handled with sensitivity and in accordance with program policies

    • When in doubt about whether to accept or give a gift, consult your supervisor or Human Resources. The goal is to maintain professional relationships free from undue influence.

Intellectual Property▼

Work products, materials, programs, curricula, and other intellectual property created by employees during the course of their employment are the property of The Children's Guild. This includes:

  • Training materials and curricula
  • Program designs and methodologies
  • Reports, analyses, and documentation
  • Software, applications, and digital content
  • Marketing and communication materials

    • Employees must:

    • Protect TCG's intellectual property from unauthorized use or disclosure
    • Not use TCG intellectual property for personal gain
    • Return all materials and work products upon separation from employment
    • Respect the intellectual property rights of others

      • Questions about intellectual property ownership should be directed to Human Resources or the Legal department.
